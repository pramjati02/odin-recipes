My Grandmother's Delicious Lasagna

There’s something about making lasagna the way my grandma did that feels like stepping into a memory—warm kitchen, bubbling sauce, and the kind of patience you don’t rush. Her recipe was never written down, just passed along by watching and tasting, a little bit of this and a handful of that. It’s rich, comforting, and layered with love as much as it is with pasta, meat, and cheese. Before we get into it, imagine slow-simmered sauce, creamy layers, and that golden, slightly crispy top fresh out of the oven—this is the kind of dish that brings everyone to the table.

Ingredients

For the meat sauce

  • 500g minced beef
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic (or 1 tsp garlic powder)
  • 1 tin (400g) chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For the Beschamel sauce

  • 50g butter
  • 50g plain flour
  • 500ml milk
  • Salt & pepper
  • Optional: pinch of nutmeg

And don't forget the layers!

  • 9–12 lasagna sheets
  • 150–200g grated mozzarella or cheddar
  • Optional: grated parmesan

Steps

  1. Cook sauce: Fry onion in oil, add garlic, then beef. Stir in tomatoes, purée, herbs, salt & pepper. Simmer 20 minutes.
  2. White sauce: Melt butter, stir in flour, slowly whisk in milk. Cook until thick, season.
  3. Layer: Meat sauce → pasta → white sauce. Repeat, finish with white sauce + cheese.
  4. Bake: 180°C (160°C fan) for 35–45 minutes. Rest 10 minutes before serving.

Serve and enjoy! Watch you and your guests melt into the flavours of this delicious lasagna.
